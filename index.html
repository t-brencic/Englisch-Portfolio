Portfolio Entry #6 ‚Äì Tap Water in Swiss Restaurants
Should it be free?
Discussion Switzerland

I believe tap water should be free in Swiss restaurants. The main reason is that when you go out to eat, you are already paying for a meal and often for other drinks too. Some fresh water from the tap should always be included as a basic service.

Switzerland has very high-quality drinking water. It makes little sense to charge extra for something that is already safe and available everywhere. Free tap water could also encourage people to drink less sugary drinks, which would be better for their health.

Personally, I would probably order more water if it was free. For someone who trains a lot and cares about performance and health, staying hydrated is essential. Having easy access to water in restaurants would support that lifestyle without making everything more expensive.

Portfolio Entry #7 ‚Äì Tariffs and Small Brands
Thinking beyond the ‚Äúcaveman‚Äù level
Tariffs Business

As someone who works with sponsors and is interested in business, I see tariffs as something that can both protect and hurt companies. Tariffs are taxes on imported goods. In theory, they can protect local businesses from cheap foreign products. But they can also make it harder for small brands to grow internationally.

For example, if a small Swiss brand wants to sell products abroad, tariffs can make their items more expensive and less attractive for customers in another country. On the other hand, if Switzerland places tariffs on foreign products, it might help local producers but also raise prices for consumers.

In my opinion, we should stop ‚Äúacting like cavemen‚Äù and only thinking in simple ‚Äúgood vs. bad‚Äù. Tariffs are a tool. The real question is how to design trade rules so that they support innovation, fair competition, and small companies ‚Äì not only big corporations.

Portfolio Entry #8 ‚Äì Deepfakes
AI videos and identity
Technology AI & Media

In this task I worked with a deepfake-style video using an AI tool (HeyGen). The idea of deepfakes is that artificial intelligence can create very realistic videos of people saying or doing things they never actually said or did.

These tools can be used for positive things, for example to create content faster, to localize marketing videos, or to make fun short clips. But they also have a dangerous side: people can use them to spread fake news, to damage someone‚Äôs reputation, or to create illegal content.

As someone active on social media and working with sponsors, deepfakes are a serious topic for me. Protecting my identity and my image online is becoming more and more important as AI tools get better.
